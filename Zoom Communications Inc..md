# Zoom Communications Inc.

## Design System v1.0

## 1. Brand Overview

- What the company is (facts)
    - Zoom Communications Inc. is an American communications technology company headquartered in San Jose, California, providing an AI‑first work platform for human connection across the Americas, Asia Pacific, Europe, the Middle East, and Africa.[^1][^2]
    - The company is best known for Zoom Meetings, offering HD video, voice, chat, and content sharing across mobile devices, desktops, laptops, telephones, and conference room systems.[^3][^4][^2]
    - Zoom’s product portfolio also includes Zoom Phone (a cloud phone system) and Zoom Team Chat, enabling persistent messaging and content sharing.[^4][^2]
    - Zoom operates in the information technology sector, specifically in software and services with a strong position in the video conferencing software developers industry.[^5][^2]
- Brand personality (derived, clearly marked)
    - Design choice (not officially published): Brand personality is defined as reliable, friendly, and modern, reflecting its role as a widely adopted, everyday collaboration tool and its use of approachable light blue as a core brand color.[^6][^7][^3]
    - Design choice (not officially published): The brand voice is clear, concise, and optimistic, emphasizing ease, frictionless communication, and human connection as core themes.[^3][^4]


## 2. Logo System

### 2.1 Primary Logo

- Fact: The Zoom primary logo consists of the word “zoom” in a custom lowercase sans-serif wordmark, set in a distinctive light “Zoom Blue” on a white background.[^6][^7]
- Fact: The construction uses simple rounded letterforms with consistent stroke thickness, contributing to a clean and contemporary appearance.[^7]
- Design choice (not officially published): For systemization, the primary logo is defined as the “Zoom wordmark” in Zoom Blue (\#2D8CFF) on white.[^6][^7]
- Design choice (not officially published): A secondary reversed logo variant is defined as the Zoom wordmark in white on a Zoom Blue (\#2D8CFF) background for use on dark or imagery backgrounds.[^6]


### 2.2 Logo Colors (HEX values)

- Fact: External brand color references identify Zoom’s primary logo color as a brilliant azure referenced as “Zoom Blue,” with HEX value \#2D8CFF.[^6][^7]

Primary logo colors (facts plus explicit usage definitions):

- Zoom Blue: `#2D8CFF` (primary brand and logo color).[^6]
- White: `#FFFFFF` (primary background and reversed logo color).[^7][^6]

Design choice (not officially published): Neutral support color for logo lockups

- Charcoal: `#1F252F` (used for text adjacent to the logo, not for the wordmark itself), selected to maintain sufficient contrast while keeping a modern, understated tone.


### 2.3 Clear Space

- Design choice (not officially published): Clear space around the primary logo is defined as at least the height of the lowercase “z” (x) on all sides.
- Design choice (not officially published): No other graphic elements, text, or edges may enter this clear space.


### 2.4 Background Usage

- Design choice (not officially published):
    - Preferred: Zoom Blue wordmark on white background (\#FFFFFF).
    - Reversed: White wordmark on Zoom Blue (\#2D8CFF) for use on dark or photographic backgrounds where legibility is otherwise compromised.
    - On light neutral backgrounds (between 5%–10% tint of charcoal, e.g. \#F4F6F9), the Zoom Blue wordmark may be used if contrast ratio remains accessible.
- Design choice (not officially published): The logo must not be placed on complex or cluttered imagery that reduces legibility; apply a soft overlay (white at 70% opacity or charcoal at 60% opacity) before placing the reversed logo.


## 3. Color System

### 3.1 Primary Colors

- Fact: Zoom’s primary identity color is a light “Zoom Blue,” described in brand literature as the unique light blue shade associated with the brand, here set as HEX \#2D8CFF.[^6][^7]

Primary palette:

- Zoom Blue: `#2D8CFF` (primary brand color)[^7][^6]
- Dark Navy: `#142033` (Design choice (not officially published): foundational UI text and background color, chosen to provide strong contrast with Zoom Blue while remaining within a tech‑corporate aesthetic.)
- White: `#FFFFFF` (core background color for presentations and product surfaces)[^7]


### 3.2 Accent Colors

Design choices (not officially published): Accent colors are defined to create a coherent system around Zoom Blue while supporting data visualization and UI feedback.

- Success Green: `#12B981` — used for success states, positive trends, and confirmations; chosen for high contrast and compatibility with blue.
- Warning Amber: `#F59E0B` — used for warnings and non‑blocking alerts.
- Error Red: `#EF4444` — used for critical errors and destructive actions.
- Soft Blue: `#E5F1FF` — a very light tint of Zoom Blue for subtle fills and backgrounds.
- Soft Gray: `#E4E7EC` — neutral backgrounds, dividers, and secondary UI surfaces.


### 3.3 Color Usage Rules

Design choices (not officially published):

- Zoom Blue (\#2D8CFF) is reserved for primary actions, key highlights, links, and key brand elements (titles, key chart series, call‑to‑action buttons).
- Dark Navy (\#142033) and charcoal‑like neutrals handle the majority of body text and icons to ensure readability and reduce blue overuse.
- Accent colors (green, amber, red) are limited to 10–15% of any layout’s visible area and used only for semantic meaning (success, warning, error) or data series differentiation.
- Backgrounds for presentations and most UI surfaces use white or very light tints (Soft Blue or Soft Gray). Strong color backgrounds are reserved for title slides or hero sections.
- Ensure a minimum contrast ratio of 4.5:1 for all essential text; when using Zoom Blue on light tints, verify contrast or switch to Dark Navy.


## 4. Typography

### 4.1 Primary Typeface (explicit choice)

- Fact: Zoom collaborated with Latinotype to create a custom neo‑grotesque font named “Happy Display” as part of its brand typography system.[^8]
- Fact: Happy Display is a proprietary typeface and may not be universally available to all designers outside Zoom.[^8]

Design choices (not officially published):

- Primary Typeface (system use):
    - Primary: **Inter** (by Rasmus Andersson) — a widely available, modern neo‑grotesque sans‑serif optimized for UI and digital products, chosen for its structural similarity to contemporary communication brands and good pairing with a rounded logo wordmark.
    - Secondary (brand headlines when Happy Display is not available): Inter with slightly increased letter spacing and heavier weights.
- Fallback stack (for digital use): `"Inter", -apple-system, BlinkMacSystemFont, "Segoe UI", system-ui, sans-serif`.


### 4.2 Hierarchy (sizes, weights)

Design choices (not officially published): Base system assumes a 16 px body size.

- Heading scale (desktop):
    - H1: 40 px, Inter Bold, line-height 1.1, for main page titles and hero headlines.
    - H2: 32 px, Inter SemiBold, line-height 1.2, for major section titles.
    - H3: 24 px, Inter SemiBold, line-height 1.3, for subsection titles.
    - H4: 20 px, Inter Medium, line-height 1.3, for minor headings or card titles.
- Body text:
    - Body 1: 16 px, Inter Regular, line-height 1.5, primary reading size.
    - Body 2: 14 px, Inter Regular, line-height 1.5, secondary text and captions.
- Emphasis:
    - Use SemiBold for emphasis rather than italics to maintain a clean, digital feel.
    - Links are Inter Regular, 16 px, in Zoom Blue (\#2D8CFF), underlined on hover.


## 5. Layout \& Grid

### 5.1 Grid System

Design choices (not officially published):

- Web and large presentation layouts:
    - 12‑column grid, 1200–1440 px content width, 24 px gutter between columns.
    - Margins: 80 px outer margin on large screens, 24 px on tablet, 16 px on mobile.
- Presentation slides (16:9):
    - Use a safe content area with 80 px margin on all sides to avoid projector cropping.
    - Invisible 4‑column grid within the safe area to align titles, content blocks, and imagery.


### 5.2 Spacing Scale

Design choices (not officially published): A modular 4‑point spacing system:

- 4, 8, 12, 16, 24, 32, 40, 56, 72 px.
- Usage rules:
    - 4–8 px: micro spacing (between labels and inputs, icon padding).
    - 12–16 px: spacing between related components (buttons, form fields).
    - 24–32 px: spacing between sections within the same module.
    - 40–72 px: page‑level padding and separation between major sections or slide blocks.


## 6. Presentation System

### 6.1 Slide Backgrounds

Design choices (not officially published):

- Default background: White `#FFFFFF`.
- Alternate brand background: Soft Blue `#E5F1FF` for sections that need light emphasis while keeping text in Dark Navy (\#142033).
- Hero or opening slides:
    - Option 1: Full Zoom Blue `#2D8CFF` with white text and white logo.
    - Option 2: White background with a Zoom Blue banner (top or left) containing the title.


### 6.2 Slide Types

Design choices (not officially published): Standardize slide templates to maintain consistency.

- Title slide:
    - Background: Zoom Blue or white.
    - Centered logo at top or top‑left, H1 title below, subtitle in Body 1.
- Section divider:
    - Background: Soft Blue with H2 title, minimal supporting text.
- Content slide (text‑heavy):
    - Two‑column layout: left column for headings and bullets, right column for diagrams or screenshots.
    - Keep line length to ~60–80 characters for readability.
- Data slide:
    - White background, charts using the defined data visualization palette.
    - Titles and key numbers in Zoom Blue, axis labels and body text in Dark Navy.
- Image‑lead slide:
    - Full‑bleed image with a dark or light overlay to maintain text legibility.
    - Title and short copy anchored to one side using the grid.


## 7. Iconography

Design choices (not officially published):

- Style:
    - Outline icons with 2 px stroke, rounded line caps and corners to visually connect with the rounded logo letterforms.
    - Minimal detail, geometric construction, and solid alignment to pixel grid for clarity at small sizes.
- Color:
    - Primary icon color: Dark Navy (\#142033) on light backgrounds.
    - When emphasizing actions or status, use Zoom Blue (\#2D8CFF) for active states, Success Green (\#12B981) for positive states, and Error Red (\#EF4444) for critical alerts.
- Sizes:
    - 16 px, 20 px, and 24 px icon sizes for UI; 48 px and 64 px for presentations and infographics.
- Usage rules:
    - Maintain consistent stroke width across all icons.
    - Avoid using full‑color illustrative icons together with outline system icons within the same layout.


## 8. Imagery Style

Design choices (not officially published):

- Subject matter:
    - Focus on real people in authentic working environments, portraying hybrid and remote collaboration: home offices, meeting rooms, and on‑the‑go work.[^3][^4]
    - Include diverse participants to reflect Zoom’s global reach.
- Style:
    - Bright, well‑lit scenes with soft shadows and a neutral to cool color temperature that complements Zoom Blue.
    - Minimal clutter in backgrounds; emphasize screens and collaboration moments.
- Illustration (if used):
    - Flat or semi‑flat vector illustrations with soft rounded corners and minimal outlines.
    - Limit palette to primary and accent colors, avoiding overly saturated gradients that clash with Zoom Blue.


## 9. Data Visualization

Design choices (not officially published):

- General:
    - Use white backgrounds with clear gridlines in Soft Gray (\#E4E7EC) at low opacity.
    - Axis labels and values in Dark Navy (\#142033); chart titles and key metrics in Zoom Blue (\#2D8CFF).
- Color mapping:
    - Series 1: Zoom Blue `#2D8CFF`.
    - Series 2: Success Green `#12B981`.
    - Series 3: Warning Amber `#F59E0B`.
    - Series 4: Error Red `#EF4444`.
    - Additional minor series: use tints of Zoom Blue and Dark Navy; avoid more than 5 simultaneous series when possible.
- Chart types:
    - Prefer line charts and bar charts for clarity; avoid 3D charts.
    - Use data labels sparingly to avoid clutter, relying on clear legends and axes.
- Accessibility:
    - Never rely on color alone to communicate meaning; use patterns, labels, or markers.
    - Ensure minimum font sizes of 12 px for chart labels in presentations.


## 10. Do / Don’t Guidelines

Design choices (not officially published):

- Do:
    - Use Zoom Blue consistently for primary actions, titles, and key highlights.
    - Maintain clear space around the logo equal to at least the height of the lowercase “z”.
    - Use the defined typography hierarchy for all presentations and digital assets.
    - Keep layouts clean with generous white space and logical grouping using the spacing scale.
    - Use imagery that reflects real collaboration and inclusive, global teams.
- Don’t:
    - Don’t stretch, distort, recolor, or add effects (shadows, outlines, gradients) to the Zoom logo.
    - Don’t place the logo on low‑contrast or noisy backgrounds without an overlay to ensure legibility.
    - Don’t introduce unapproved colors or additional primary hues that compete with Zoom Blue.
    - Don’t mix multiple typefaces beyond the defined primary stack in the same layout.
    - Don’t overload slides or pages with more than one primary focal point; avoid dense text blocks without visual hierarchy.


## 11. File Naming \& Handoff Notes

Design choices (not officially published):

- File naming conventions:
    - Brand assets: `zoom_brand-[asset-type]-[version]-[color]-[size].[ext]`
        - Example: `zoom_brand-logo-primary-blue-rgb-1024px.png`
        - Example: `zoom_brand-logo-reversed-white-on-blue-svg.svg`
    - Presentation templates: `zoom_presentation-[use-case]-v[major.minor].[ext]`
        - Example: `zoom_presentation-sales-deck-v1.0.pptx`
    - Icon sets: `zoom_icons-[style]-[size]-v[version].[ext]`
        - Example: `zoom_icons-outline-24px-v1.0.svg`
- Handoff notes:
    - Provide both vector (SVG, PDF) and raster (PNG @1x, @2x, @3x) versions of logos in primary and reversed variants.
    - Include a color tokens document specifying HEX values and their semantic roles (primary, background, success, warning, error).
    - Deliver typography styles as reusable text styles in Figma, Sketch, or the team’s chosen design tool, following the defined hierarchy.
    - Attach this Design System v1.0 as part of the brand kit and update versioning whenever changes are made to core colors, typography, or logo usage rules.
